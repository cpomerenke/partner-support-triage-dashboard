Referral Partnerships — Partner Experience Audit

Partner Experience
Is a Revenue Risk.

This report quantifies the volume, intensity and outcome risk of partner and client escalations reaching the Partnerships function in 2026. The data makes the case that service issues are directly threatening the referral relationships on which growth depends.

Data Scope — Important Caveat
This report captures escalations visible in Gmail and the #partner-experience-and-support Slack channel only. It does not account for phone-based support conversations, direct Slack messages exchanged with internal colleagues, internal comments added directly to Zendesk tickets, or any escalation managed outside these two channels. The true volume and time cost is therefore materially higher than what is presented here — the figures in this report should be read as a conservative floor, not a complete picture.

Channel Picture

This data represents one Partner Manager in Australia managing approximately 150 partners. The channel has 6–7 Partner Managers at any given time. Based on the first half of 2026 alone, if this volume continues and holds across the channel, the referral function is on track to absorb 2,000–2,400+ escalations annually and 1,100–1,400+ hours of untracked support work per year — workload that has never been formally quantified or put to leadership for resourcing consideration.

02 — Avoidability: What Shouldn't Reach Partnerships

The Partnerships function is acting as a de facto escalation path for support failures. Of 170 incidents, only 30 (18%) were cases where Partnerships involvement was appropriate. The remaining 140 incidents — representing 415 touchpoints and ~78 hours — were either avoidable or only partially warranted.

Structural Pattern
Partnerships Acting as Informal Help Desk
A notable pattern in this data is the volume of issues that are being raised — and resolved — via the Partner Manager posting in the #partner-experience-and-support Slack channel on behalf of partners and clients. This is the partner manager acting as an intermediary because the formal support channel is not producing timely or satisfactory outcomes. These interactions are unrecorded in any ticketing system, sit outside the defined scope of the Partnerships role, and consume time that should be directed at revenue-generating activity. The 32 Slack-logged incidents in this report represent only the interactions that were formally captured — the full volume of Slack-mediated support is higher.

05 — Time Estimate Methodology

The 95.4 hours figure is a conservative floor. It does not include time spent on phone calls, verbal conversations with colleagues, or investigations conducted outside of email and Slack. Many incidents — particularly high-severity cases — involved significant background research, internal conversations, and follow-up that is not reflected in message counts. The true time cost is higher.

How the Hours Were Calculated
Each incident was assigned to one of three time bands based on thread depth, issue complexity, and severity.
Low — 20 minutes
~82 incidents = 27.3 hrs
Single-touch queries with a clear, straightforward answer. Short email threads (1–3 messages), Slack-only questions with known resolutions, and routine admin requests (e.g. BP access checks, phone PIN queries).
Medium — 45 minutes
~63 incidents = 47.4 hrs
Multi-touch incidents requiring investigation, liaison with another team, or drafting a considered response. Includes billing queries, implementation check-ins, and delayed support ticket follow-ups with 4–10 message threads.
High — 90 minutes
~14 incidents = 20.7 hrs
Complex, high-stakes incidents involving churn risk, implementation failure, or multi-party coordination. Typically involves background research, internal escalation, multiple rounds of communication, and relationship management. 90 minutes is itself a floor for several of these cases.
Total: 95.4 hours — Low (27.3) + Medium (47.4) + High (20.7) — across 24 weeks, equivalent to 4.0 hours per week or 2.5 full working weeks absorbed by escalation management alone.
Why This Is Conservative
Factors not captured in the estimate.
Phone calls — several incidents involved verbal conversations with partners or internal stakeholders not reflected in email counts
Internal investigation time — researching account history, checking platform configs, reviewing tickets, and liaising with Support before replying
Zendesk internal comments — time spent adding notes directly to support tickets is not captured in this audit
DM conversations — direct Slack messages with colleagues coordinating on partner issues are outside the scope of this report
Context switching — the cognitive cost of moving between escalation management and revenue-generating activities is real but unquantifiable
The Real Number
95.4 hours is what can be evidenced. The actual time investment is meaningfully higher — particularly for the 18 high-severity incidents, each of which likely consumed several hours of background work before a single email was sent.
